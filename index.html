O pitiful worm with a coward’s grin,
Too weak to play, so you choose to sin.

So you crawl like a rat, and you stoop to cheat.

Spineless trickster, hollow and lame,
Your hands reach the prize, but you earn no name.
A thief of riddles, a beggar of lore,
You grasp at the crumbs that the worthy ignore.

No honor, no cunning — just empty disgrace,
A slug in the dust, with mud on your face.
While true minds triumph, sharp and bright,
You’ll wallow in shadows, unfit for the fight.
